Operating controls

Built for consulting guardrails.

Designed for client work from the first line. Three controls keep it fast, in your hands, and confidential.

✓ Confidential

Client material stays local

Client-confidential and NDA/MSA content lives only in a cache on your own machine — a hard "never goes outward" rule. Nothing is posted or sent without you.

✓ In your hands

Read-only by default

Every command reads and drafts; nothing is sent, posted, accepted, or shared until you approve it. Approving one action is never standing approval for the next.

✓ Well-behaved

Local-first Microsoft 365

It caches your calendar and mail and refreshes only when stale — instant to use, and it won't hammer Microsoft Graph or trip throttling limits.

⚠ Check first: confirm with your leadership / IT that Claude Code with the Microsoft 365 connector is approved for your account before wiring it to work mail. This is a personal-productivity setup — not an official Cognizant product.

Questions

Before you start.

Do I need to be technical?+
No. You install one app and paste one prompt. The interview is a normal conversation, and Claude explains each thing it builds in plain language. If you can use Outlook, you can use this.
What data does it touch?+
Your Microsoft 365 — calendar, mail, and (if connected) Teams/SharePoint — read through the connector you authorize. It keeps a local cache on your own machine for speed, and treats client-confidential / NDA material as something that never leaves your machine without your explicit approval.
Will it email or message people on its own?+
No. It's read-only by default. It drafts replies, reports, and prep locally and shows them to you; nothing is sent, posted, or accepted until you say so — every time.
Do I need approval to use it?+
Check with your leadership / IT that Claude Code with the Microsoft 365 connector is approved for your account before wiring it to work mail. Treat this as a personal-productivity setup, not an official Cognizant product.
How is it different from just chatting with an AI?+
Two things: a persistent memory that learns your engagements and preferences across sessions, and commands wired to your real M365 data. Instead of re-explaining your context every time, you say "/prep my 2pm" and it already knows the players, the history, and the open risks.
What if it gets something wrong?+
Correct it, then run /improve. It writes the lesson to memory so it won't repeat the mistake. The system is built to get better the more you steer it.
Can I customise the commands?+
Yes — run /skill-center to add, edit, or audit commands as your needs change. The starter set is just a foundation.
