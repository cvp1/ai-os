Command reference

Your toolkit, in plain language.

Type a slash-command and it does the work — reading from a fast local cache of your mail & calendar, drafting locally, and sending nothing without your explicit OK.

Command
What it does
Try saying
/brief
Your morning brief — today's meetings and who's in them, what's waiting on you, top priorities, deadlines this week.
"/brief" → your whole day on one screen before the first call.
/triage
One pass over your inbox + calendar. Sorts mail into Needs Reply / FYI / Noise, flags conflicts. Read-only until you say otherwise.
"/triage my inbox" → Monday's backlog already sorted.
/prep
Preps you for one meeting — the invite, the mail thread, the relevant project & people notes, last decisions, open items. Hands you an agenda and likely questions.
"/prep my 2pm with the design team" → walk in ready.
/status
Drafts a status update from your notes + recent activity: what progressed, what's blocked, decisions needed, next steps. Polished or direct — your call.
"/status for <project>" → a draft you edit & send.
/weekly
Pre-builds your weekly review — what shipped, decisions, what slipped or is blocked, next week's shape — plus a ready-to-edit status update.
"/weekly" on Friday → review's already written.
/wiki
Precedent search over your own notes — "what did I decide about X?" — answered with citations to the exact note and heading.
"/wiki the data-migration decision"
/improve
The learning loop. Captures what you corrected this session into durable memory, so the system gets more "you" every time you steer it.
"/improve" after you fix its tone → it sticks.
/ingest
Turns raw notes you drop in — clippings, transcripts, meeting exports — into maintained, linked concept pages you can navigate, instead of re-summarizing the same sources every time.
"/ingest my reading" → a living knowledge base, not a pile.

Plus /memory-prune to keep your memory healthy, and /skill-center — a meta-command to build and refine new commands as your needs grow.

Operating controls

Built with guardrails.

Designed to be trustworthy from the first line. Three controls keep it fast, in your hands, and private.

✓ Private

Your data stays local

Your mail, calendar, and notes live only in a cache on your own machine — a hard "never goes outward" rule. Nothing is posted or sent without you.

✓ In your hands

Read-only by default

Every command reads and drafts; nothing is sent, posted, accepted, or shared until you approve it. Approving one action is never standing approval for the next.

✓ Well-behaved

Local-first by design

It caches your calendar and mail and refreshes only when stale — instant to use, and it won't hammer Microsoft Graph or the Gmail API or trip rate limits.

⚠ Check first: confirm with your leadership / IT that Claude Code with the Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace connector is approved for your account before wiring it to work mail. This is a personal-productivity setup — not an official product of any employer.

Questions

Before you start.

Do I need to be technical?+
No. You install one app and paste one prompt. The interview is a normal conversation, and Claude explains each thing it builds in plain language. If you can use email and a calendar, you can use this.
What data does it touch?+
Your mail and calendar — Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace — read through the connector you authorize. It keeps a local cache on your own machine for speed, and treats anything you mark confidential as something that never leaves your machine without your explicit approval.
Will it email or message people on its own?+
No. It's read-only by default. It drafts replies, updates, and prep locally and shows them to you; nothing is sent, posted, or accepted until you say so — every time.
Do I need approval to use it?+
If you're connecting a work account, check with your leadership / IT that Claude Code with the Microsoft 365 or Google connector is approved first. Treat this as a personal-productivity setup, not an official product of any employer.
How is it different from just chatting with an AI?+
Two things: a persistent memory that learns your projects and preferences across sessions, and commands wired to your real mail & calendar. Instead of re-explaining your context every time, you say "/prep my 2pm" and it already knows the players, the history, and what's open.
What if it gets something wrong?+
Correct it, then run /improve. It writes the lesson to memory so it won't repeat the mistake. The system is built to get better the more you steer it.
Can I customise the commands?+
Yes — run /skill-center to add, edit, or audit commands as your needs change. The starter set is just a foundation.
