## What it does
`/learning` is a standing strategist for everything you're trying to learn. It keeps a living portfolio of your goals and a prioritized, prerequisite-ordered topic queue across all of them, drafts a roadmap for a goal, and tells you what's gone cold and worth revisiting. **It plans; `/teach` teaches** — it hands each topic to `/teach` (the tutor that already ships with your AI-OS) and never runs a lesson itself. Everything lives in `~/ai-os/learning/`, so it shares your memory and `/recall`.

## When to reach for it
When you're trying to learn several things at once and want an order that respects prerequisites and your time, instead of learning at random — and a standing "what should I pick up next, and what have I let go cold?" Opt-in second act; hire it after your first win.

## A walkthrough
Setup interviews you about your whole learning *portfolio* — what you want to be able to *do* (concrete outcomes), why each matters, what you already know, and how much time you realistically have — then reads back a suggested order and asks "anything wrong or missing?" The first win is a roadmap that ends by handing you off to `/teach`:

> **You:** /learning roadmap for "ship a small web app"
> **/learning:** *Prioritized roadmap (needs-X-first noted):*
> 1. HTML/CSS basics — *the surface you'll build on*
> 2. JavaScript fundamentals — *needs #1*
> 3. **START HERE** → a first small page…
> When you're ready, run `/teach me HTML/CSS basics` — it keeps a syllabus and remembers where you struggle.

- **`/learning roadmap`** — drafts a prioritized, prerequisite-ordered roadmap for a goal, ending in a `/teach` handoff.
- **`/learning review`** — a weekly "what's gone cold and is worth revisiting."
- **`/learning status`** — where your learning portfolio stands.
- **`/learning decide`** — record what to start, pause, or drop.
- **`/learning onboard`** / **`/learning research`** — refine goals later; find roadmap-level sources.

## Power user & honest limits
- **The clean seam:** the manager owns the portfolio *across* topics (what to learn, in what order); `/teach` owns everything *inside* a topic (the syllabus, the lessons, your progress). The manager reads `/teach`'s progress to spot cold topics; it never writes inside a topic's lesson workspace or builds a syllabus.
- **`/learning review` is a weekly "what to revisit," not per-card spaced repetition** — it's a pull-based digest, not an optimal-interval reminder system.
- **No `/teach` yet?** It'll offer to walk you through the first topic once — it never builds its own tutor.
- **No connector needed; nothing to send** — it's a planner over plain files you own.
